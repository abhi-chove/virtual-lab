Overview:-

The aim of this experiment is the identification of unknown compounds through a combination of spectrometer methods.
1. Identification of unknown components using spectroscopic techniques involves analyzing the interaction between the component and electromagnetic radiation. Spectroscopic techniques include a range of methods such as infrared spectroscopy, ultraviolet-visible spectroscopy, nuclear magnetic resonance spectroscopy, mass spectrometry, and X-ray crystallography.
2. In general, spectroscopic techniques can provide valuable information about the composition, structure, and properties of unknown components. By comparing the spectroscopic data of an unknown component to a database of known compounds, it is often possible to identify the component. Additionally, spectroscopic techniques can be used in combination with other analytical techniques, such as chromatography or elemental analysis, to further identify unknown components.
